SecureBank Internal Policy Document

Mortgage Conditions:
To qualify for a standard mortgage at SecureBank, the applicant must have a minimum credit score of 700. The maximum loan term allowed is 25 years. The standard fixed interest rate for 2026 is 5%

Account Overdraft Rules:
If a customer's account balance falls below $0, a standard overdraft fee of $35 will be applied immediately. SecureBank does not waive overdraft fees under any circumstances.

Investment Restrictions:
SecureBank strictly prohibits its advisors from recommending or facilitating the purchase of cryptocurrencies, meme stocks, or high-frequency day trading algorithms. Advisors must only recommend approved mutual funds and government bonds.

Personal Loan Policy:
SecureBank offers personal loans to existing customers who have maintained an active account in good standing for a minimum of 12 consecutive months. Applicants must have a minimum FICO credit score of 680, a debt-to-income (DTI) ratio not exceeding 40%, and a verifiable annual income of at least $24,000. Loan amounts range from $2,500 to $50,000, with repayment terms between 12 and 60 months. The standard fixed Annual Percentage Rate (APR) for personal loans begins at 8.99% for well-qualified borrowers and may be as high as 21.99% depending on creditworthiness. Origination fees of 1% of the loan amount apply to all approved personal loans.

Savings Account Interest Rates:
SecureBank offers two tiers of savings accounts. The Standard Savings Account pays a variable Annual Percentage Yield (APY) of 0.50% on all balances. The Premier Savings Account, available to customers maintaining a minimum daily balance of $10,000, pays a variable APY of 3.75%. Interest is calculated daily and credited to the account on the last business day of each calendar month. SecureBank reserves the right to adjust savings APYs with 30 days' written notice to account holders. All savings account balances are FDIC-insured up to $250,000 per depositor.

Wire Transfer Policy:
SecureBank charges a flat fee of $25 for outgoing domestic wire transfers and $45 for outgoing international wire transfers. Incoming wire transfers, whether domestic or international, are subject to a $15 processing fee. Wire transfers submitted before 3:00 PM Eastern Time on a business day will be processed the same day; submissions after that cutoff will be processed the following business day. International wire transfers may be subject to additional intermediary bank fees outside SecureBank's control. The daily outgoing wire transfer limit per account is $250,000. SecureBank does not waive wire transfer fees under standard account terms.

Credit Card Late Payment Fee Policy:
SecureBank credit cardholders who fail to make at least the minimum required payment by the statement due date will be assessed a late payment fee of $29 for the first occurrence in any 12-month rolling period, and $40 for each subsequent late payment within the same 12-month window. Late fees are applied to the account on the business day following the missed due date. In addition to the late fee, the account's Annual Percentage Rate (APR) may be increased to a penalty APR of up to 29.99% if two or more late payments occur within any 12-month period. Cardholders are encouraged to enroll in AutoPay to avoid late payment fees. SecureBank does not waive late payment fees except in cases of documented bank error.

ATM Withdrawal Policy:
SecureBank operates a network of over 2,000 fee-free ATMs nationwide, identified by the SecureBank logo. Withdrawals made at any SecureBank-branded ATM are free of charge for all account holders. For withdrawals at out-of-network ATMs, SecureBank charges a $3.00 convenience fee per transaction in addition to any surcharge imposed by the ATM operator. Customers with a Premier Checking account are reimbursed up to $15 in out-of-network ATM fees per calendar month. The daily ATM cash withdrawal limit is $500 for Standard Checking accounts and $1,000 for Premier Checking accounts. International ATM withdrawals are subject to a 2% foreign currency conversion fee on top of the standard out-of-network fee.

Certificate of Deposit (CD) Policy:
SecureBank offers fixed-rate Certificates of Deposit with terms of 3, 6, 12, 24, and 36 months. The minimum opening deposit for any CD is $1,000. Current fixed APYs are: 3-month CD at 3.50%, 6-month CD at 4.00%, 12-month CD at 4.25%, 24-month CD at 4.10%, and 36-month CD at 3.90%. All CD rates are locked at the time of opening and do not change for the duration of the term. Upon maturity, a CD will automatically renew for the same term at the prevailing rate unless the customer provides written or in-app notice during the 7-calendar-day grace period following maturity. Early withdrawal prior to maturity results in a penalty equal to 90 days' interest for terms of 12 months or less, and 180 days' interest for terms exceeding 12 months.

Account Closure Policy:
Customers may close a SecureBank deposit account at any time by submitting a written request in-branch, by certified mail, or through the SecureBank secure messaging portal. Accounts closed within 90 days of opening are subject to an early closure fee of $25. Accounts with an outstanding negative balance must be brought to $0 or above before a closure request will be processed. Any accrued interest will be paid out upon account closure. Accounts that remain inactive (no customer-initiated transactions) for 12 consecutive months will be assessed a $10 monthly dormancy fee beginning in the 13th month. SecureBank reserves the right to close any account with seven (7) days' written notice in cases of repeated policy violations or suspected fraudulent activity.

Joint Account Policy:
SecureBank permits a maximum of two (2) account holders on any joint deposit account. Both applicants must be at least 18 years of age, present a valid government-issued photo ID, and provide a U.S. Social Security Number or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. All joint account holders possess equal and independent access rights, including the ability to deposit, withdraw, or transfer funds without the consent of the co-owner. Either account holder may request account closure independently; however, both parties will be notified in writing within two (2) business days of such a request. Each co-owner's deposits are separately insured by the FDIC up to $250,000. SecureBank does not offer joint accounts in conjunction with minor or custodial account products.

Student Account Policy:
SecureBank's Student Checking Account is available to full-time or part-time students enrolled at an accredited college, university, or vocational institution who are between the ages of 17 and 26. Eligible students must provide current proof of enrollment (such as a student ID or enrollment letter) at account opening and annually thereafter. The Student Checking Account carries no monthly maintenance fee, no minimum balance requirement, and no overdraft fee for the first overdraft occurrence per calendar year; the standard $35 overdraft fee applies from the second occurrence onward. Students receive free access to all SecureBank ATMs and are reimbursed up to $10 in out-of-network ATM fees per month. The Student Checking Account automatically converts to a Standard Checking Account at age 27 or upon loss of verified student status, at which point standard fee schedules apply.

Foreign Transaction Fee Policy:
SecureBank assesses a foreign transaction fee of 3% of the transaction amount on all debit card and credit card purchases where the merchant's bank is located outside the United States, regardless of the currency used. This fee applies to in-person transactions abroad as well as online purchases from internationally based merchants. International ATM withdrawals made on a SecureBank debit card are subject to the 3% foreign transaction fee in addition to the standard $3.00 out-of-network ATM fee. Customers holding a SecureBank Premier Travel Credit Card are exempt from the foreign transaction fee on all purchases. Foreign transaction fees are non-refundable and are posted to the account within two (2) business days of the transaction settlement date.
